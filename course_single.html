About Course

GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering) is a national-level entrance exam in India used for admission to Postgraduate (PG) programs like M.Tech., M.E., M.S., and for Ph.D. programs in top Indian institutes such as IITs, NITs, IIITs. It is also widely used for recruitment in Public Sector Units (PSUs) like IOCL, BHEL, ONGC, and more.

Here is a breakdown regarding GATE for PG & Ph.D. courses:

Purpose of GATE

  • Admission to M.Tech./M.E./MS in top engineering institutes.
  • Admission to Ph.D. programs in Engineering, Technology, and Sciences.
  • Recruitment in PSUs such as BHEL, ONGC, IOCL, and NTPC.
  • Some international universities (e.g., NTU Singapore) accept GATE scores.
  • Eligibility for AICTE/MHRD scholarships during PG/Ph.D. studies.

Eligibility

  • Final-year or completed Bachelor's degree (B.E./B.Tech./B.Arch./B.Sc./B.Pharm).
  • Final-year or completed Master's degree in Science, Math, Stats, or Computer Applications.
  • No age limit or restriction on number of attempts.

GATE Exam Details

  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Total: 65 questions (100 marks)
  • Types: MCQs, MSQs, and Numerical Answer Types
  • General Aptitude (15 marks) + Subject Paper (85 marks)

Score Validity & Financial Support

  • GATE score is valid for 3 years for admissions.
  • Stipend for PG students: Rs.12,400/month
  • Stipend for Ph.D. students: approx. Rs.31,000/month
